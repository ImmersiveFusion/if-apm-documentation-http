# Persona: AI/Tech Influencer

## Profile

- **Role:** YouTuber, Tech Blogger, AI Enthusiast
- **Experience:** Covers emerging tech, AI tools, novel interfaces
- **Context:** Looking for interesting AI + developer tools to feature

## Background

Covers AI developments for a broad tech audience. Always looking for "the future of X" content. Has featured tools like Cursor, Copilot, and various AI-powered dev tools. Audience expects demos, not just descriptions. Values "wow factor" but also practical utility.

## Skepticism & Concerns

- "AI Copilot sounds like everyone else. What's different?"
- "Will this make a good video or is it boring to watch?"
- "Is the AI actually useful or just a chatbot wrapper?"
- "Can I show something impressive in under 5 minutes?"
- "Is this vaporware or does it actually work?"

## What They Need

### For Content
- Visual demo opportunities
- Impressive AI capabilities
- Quotable features/claims
- Comparison to familiar tools
- "Future of work" narrative

### To Recommend
- Actually works as shown
- Accessible to their audience
- Worth their credibility
- Not overhyped

## Critical Questions

1. "What can the AI Copilot do that ChatGPT + my APM can't?"
2. "Can the AI actually find root causes or just summarize data?"
3. "Is the voice interface gimmicky or genuinely useful?"
4. "Show me the most impressive thing the AI can do."
5. "What's the AI trained on? My data? Generic knowledge?"

## Evaluation Prompts

1. "I'm making a video called 'I Debugged Production in VR with AI.' Will it deliver?"
2. "My audience loves AI demos. What's your most viral-worthy feature?"
3. "Compare your AI to Copilot for debugging. Be specific."
4. "I want to show the AI finding a bug I couldn't. Can you set that up?"
5. "What's the AI's failure mode? When does it give wrong answers?"

## Success Criteria

- Provides demo-worthy capabilities
- AI delivers on promises
- Content is shareable and engaging
- Honest about AI limitations
- Makes them look good for recommending it
